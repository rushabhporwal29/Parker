PARKER SHIRTS & TROUSERS is a common man’s brand established by Mr. Shrikant Joshi & Mr. Virendra Joshi in the year 1989 in Pune. It is engaged in manufacturing and marketing of PARKER products. They manufacture shirts and trousers of different varieties in their production unit named as MAHALAXMI APPARELS . They have a total of five production units in Maharashtra and have nearby 30 outlets in and around the state.

Their main aim is to satisfy the middle class consumers by selling good quality garments at reasonable rates. They inaugurated their first outlet in Pune in which they highlighted the rates of the products which were incomparable to the current market price. Good quality shirts were introduced from the range of Rs. 60 onwards and Trousers from Rs.80 onwards. This move helps them gain a large market share and establish their presence in Pune. Within a year they inaugurated the second mega outlet in the market place of Pune (Laxmi road). They kept a low margin and aimed for a higher volume of customers. This strategy was consistently used which resulted in improved customer loyalty. This response encouraged the company to spread it branches all over Maharashtra.

Recently they inaugurated their 30th showroom in Dehradun with an exclusive variety and got a good response.

The company manufactures a wide range of shirts which consists of distinct fabrics.

  1. Tere-cotton
  2. Polyester
  3. Linen
  4. Pure cotton

The fabrics used for the production of trousers are as follows:-

  1. Tere-wool
  2. Mattie
  3. Cotton
  4. Jute

The company also focuses some of its products at the younger class of customers by offering semi casual range of clothing. The company offers a range of different designs in the clothing which appeal to the choice of the target market.

Following are some of the Terms and conditions which Parker has Benchmarked for itself and follows it religiously:-

  1. Using quality raw material from renowned vendors like Arvind Mills, Gems textiles, Century Mills etc.
  2. Provide replacements in-case of any defects even after wash.
  3. Selling products at consistent rates across all the outlets
  4. The code of conduct adopted by the company for retailers/franchisees.
    1. The company provides credit limit of two months to their retailers.
    2. They also accept the responsibility for damaged goods and provide replacement with immediate effect.
    3. The unsold or outdated products are replaced with more currently appropriate products.
    4. The designs and outlook of the stores is decided by the company which brings consistency and maintain the theme of the brand.
    5. The company also organizes promotional activities in the surrounding of various outlets without charging anything from the retailers such as hoarding displays, leaflet distributions, paper adds, local TV adds etc.
    6. They do not charge any kind of royalty on the retailers for selling their products.
    7. They charge a reasonable amount of deposits which is refundable in case of termination of contract.

Parker manufactures distinct varieties in garments at reasonable rates with branded quality and finishing. The quality of the fabric is rich and durable. Recently they have also introduced pure terry wool trousers and 100 percent cotton shirt in their outlets. A customer having low budget and also a luxurious customer can get satisfied with the present variety.

For the retailers it can be the best option for franchising Parker as it has set a distinct image in consumers mind. It can be concluded by research that this brand offers good quality at low price in the market. The other plus point is that they do not charge any kind of royalty and gives more than 25 % of margin to the retailers.

It is good opportunity for the new entrepreneur as it reduces the Capital expenditure.

They also gives a wide selection of clothing’s to the retailers and the products are supplied according to their preferences and according to the trend which is followed in the particular area. For e.g. the goods supplied to Karad or Sangli store is different as compared to the goods sold in Pune or Dehradun outlets. Hence this shows the variation in the taste of the consumers in various regions.
